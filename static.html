About the Book

Published in 2003, The Da Vinci Code follows Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon and cryptologist Sophie Neveu as they unravel a series of cryptic clues hidden in famous artworks and historical landmarks. Their quest to uncover a profound secret guarded by the enigmatic Priory of Sion takes them on a heart-pounding adventure across Europe, challenging everything we thought we knew about history and religion.

Daniel Gerhard Brown is an American author best known for his thriller novels, including the Robert Langdon novels Angels & Demons, The Da Vinci Code, The Lost Symbol, Inferno, and Origin. His novels are treasure hunts that usually take place over a period of 24 hours.

Born: June 22, 1964 (age 60 years), Exeter, New Hampshire, United States

Meet the Characters

Exploring the Themes

The Da Vinci Code delves into numerous thought-provoking themes, including:

  • The Intersection of Science and Religion
    • The novel challenges readers to consider the coexistence of faith and reason, prompting discussions on how these two realms can complement or conflict with each other.
  • The Power of Symbols
    • Symbols play a pivotal role in the story, reminding us of their profound influence throughout history and their ability to convey complex ideas and hidden truths.
  • The Role of Women in History
    • Through the character of Sophie Neveu and the revelations about Mary Magdalene, the book invites us to reconsider the often overlooked contributions of women in shaping our world.
